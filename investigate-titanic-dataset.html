I am starting off with a function that will allow me to do some basic comparison

def comparisons(main_series,str2):
    compare_series=titanic_df[main_series]
    compare_series_count=compare_series[str2].value_counts()
    total_series_count=titanic_df[str2].value_counts()
    print ("Total count")
    print (total_series_count)
    print (compare_series_count)
    print (compare_series_count/total_series_count)
    return (compare_series_count/total_series_count)

Conclusion

Several factors seemed to have had contributed to the outcome of surviving the titanic. The most predominant are: Being a female -- 74% of females survived Being a first class passenger -- 63% of 1st class passengers survived Passengers who boarded on Cherbourg port-- 55% passenger who embarked from this port survived Having family on board -- 51% of passengers who had parents or children on board survived and 46% of passenger who had spouse or siblings survived Regarding age (at first glance, just looking at the graph), most survivors seemed to have been between the ages of 20 and 40 which is usually considered a healthy age and makes sense why most survivors were in this age range, but also most of the passengers on board were in this age range.

This is only a sample of the population on board of the titanic so results would most likely be different if we were to analyze the data of all the passengers on board as well as being more accurate. This data only shows 891 passengers with some information missing, while the total number of passengers and crew that was calculated to be on board was 2,224. Some tests that could be useful to include are corelational tests and hypothesis testing to confirm the relationship of some variables to survival.
