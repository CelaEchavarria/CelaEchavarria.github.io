Reflection

I started by exploring the variables that I thought might affect the ranking of red wines. I then followed to plot the quality variable against the rest to check for any obvious relationship graphically. I performed correlation test to confirm any correlation. Alcohol and Volatile Acidity were the only ones that showed some correlation, although not strong. Since I found some correlation with volatile acidity I proceeded to explore the other acid-related variables and found correlation between volatile acidity and citric acid, and fixed acidity and citric acid. Total sulfur dioxide didn’t have a strong correlation with quality according to the results of the correlation test, but I still wanted to explore it since it included free sulfur dioxide and this variable affects the smell and flavor of wine. After exploring volatile acidity with citric acid, I saw that although not a very strong correlation graphically, most wines with higher quality fell between 0.25 and 0.50g/dm^3 of citric acid, so this might be an indication that this is the perfect range of citric acid which affects the freshness and flavor of the wine. After exploring total sulfur dioxide and free sulfur dioxide, I definitely saw a correlation which wasn’t surprising. Wines 7 and 8 seemed to be more contracted on the lowers left corner of the graph, which indicates lower total sulfur dioxide and lower free sulfur dioxide. We notice that there are no wines ranked 8 that have higher than 50mg/dm^3 of free sulfur dioxide which is the level that taste and smell start being noticeable. However, rankings 3 and 4 are also under 50mg/dm^3 of free sulfur dioxide.

The difficulties in this analysis was to interpret the graphs with so many outliers, relate the variables that intuitively should relate to the ranking of the wines but then after some test and graphically representations, not see any clear relationship. There seem to be more playing into account when it comes for the experts to rank wines. The description of the data did mention that these were the variables they thought would be related to the quality, therefore there might be other features that are not included in the dataset that would affect the quality of the wine. Perhaps with more observation a better conclusion on what makes a wine great could be drawn but from these variables given it is difficult to draw a conclusion. Other variables such as year and region could also help compare among rankings and how the variables differ by year and region.
